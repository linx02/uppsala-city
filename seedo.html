See & Do

Domkyrkan

Uppsala Domkyrka, also known as Uppsala Cathedral, is a magnificent Gothic-style cathedral located in Uppsala, Sweden. It is the largest church in Scandinavia and serves as the seat of the Archbishop of Uppsala, the head of the Church of Sweden. With its rich history dating back to the 13th century, Uppsala Domkyrka is a symbol of Swedish religious and cultural heritage. The cathedral's stunning architecture, adorned with intricate stone carvings and majestic spires, captivates visitors from around the world. Inside, the cathedral houses beautiful chapels, tombs of Swedish kings and queens, and an awe-inspiring altarpiece. Uppsala Domkyrka stands as a testament to Sweden's spiritual and architectural legacy, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in its grandeur and serenity.

Botanic garden

Uppsala Botanic Garden is a picturesque oasis located in Uppsala, Sweden. Covering a vast area, it is one of the oldest and most renowned botanical gardens in the country. The garden offers a serene escape with its meticulously curated collection of plants and flowers from various regions of the world. Visitors can stroll through enchanting pathways, lined with vibrant blooms, lush greenery, and peaceful ponds. Uppsala Botanic Garden serves as an educational and research hub, providing valuable insights into plant biodiversity and conservation. It features themed sections, such as the rock garden, herb garden, and arboretum, each showcasing a distinct array of plant species. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, a botany lover, or simply seeking tranquility, Uppsala Botanic Garden is a delightful destination that promises a harmonious blend of beauty and botanical knowledge.
